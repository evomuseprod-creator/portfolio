The house

Anchored by a concrete spine and wrapped in timber screens, it lives quietly, and opens to the garden.

Designed in 2021 as a home for one family, Belmont House sets a calm, almost monastic ground floor against a canopy of mature trees. Light is the material that changes through the day.

Every room looks onto green. The plan keeps the street at arm's length and turns the living, the pool and the bedrooms inward, toward a planted courtyard at the heart of the house.

01 / The living space

A double-height room that borrows the garden.

The main living volume rises the full height of the house, glazed on two sides so the rain trees feel like part of the room. A board-formed concrete wall holds the fireplace and the stair.

03 / Material & craft

Concrete, oak and stone, left to be themselves.

The palette is short and honest: board-formed concrete, solid oak joinery and travertine underfoot. Nothing is veneered, and the details are made to age well rather than to impress on day one.
